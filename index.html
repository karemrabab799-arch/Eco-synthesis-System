Eco-Synthesis System

Sustainable resource management and human life support on the Red Planet through advanced recycling technologies

Smart Sorting and PrecisionRouting (Detection)

This phase ensures zero cross-contamination. A High-Resolution Spectrometer instantly analyzes the chemical composition of the incoming mission waste (polymers, fabrics, light metals). Based on the analysis, a precision robotic arm directs the material to its corresponding pre-processing channel (e.g., thermal decomposition for plastics or shredding for fibers). This step is vital for optimizing the material for the subsequent chemical bond

Stage Features:

  • Operates autonomously using AI systems
  • Designed for Mars' low-gravity conditions
  • Powered by abundant solar energy
  • Reduces dependence on Earth supplies

Thermo-Chemical Synthesis(Processing)

This is the core ISRU function. The pre-processed waste is combined with a measured amount of Martian Regolith (filtered Martian soil) inside the central chamber. The unit then applies High Thermal Energy and Pressure (HTP) to force a chemical bond between the waste residue and the Regolith's iron-oxide compounds. This process creates a single, structurally homogenous composite material: Trans-Regolith.

Stage Features:

  • Advanced chemical processing systems
  • Optimized for thin atmosphere conditions
  • Solar-powered energy efficient design
  • Closed-loop resource utilization

Extrusion and Fabrication prep(Output)

The final stage prepares the new resource for the crew. The cooled Trans-Regolith Composite is channeled into a precision extrusion unit. The unit draws and spools the material into standardized, robust 3D Printing Filament. This finished filament is immediately ready for Additive Manufacturing (3D printing) of tools, spares, and habitat components, effectively closing the mission's resource loop and ensuring self-sufficiency.

Stage Features:

  • Advanced 3D printing capabilities
  • Tools and parts manufacturing
  • Construction materials production
  • Local resource independence

Recycling Stages Overview

Sorting

Initial waste separation stage using advanced robotic systems and sensors designed for Martian conditions.

Synthesis

Transformation of waste into usable raw materials through advanced biochemical processes adapted for Mars.

Fabrication

Manufacturing of essential products using recycled materials with advanced 3D printing technologies.
